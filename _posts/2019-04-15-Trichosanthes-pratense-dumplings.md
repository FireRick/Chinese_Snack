# Trichosanthes pratense dumplings

![Trichosanthes pratense dumplings]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/451183.jpg)

# Introduction

**"In March of Yangchun, it's delicious but not wild vegetable, and koji grass is one of them. Today, it's used to make small dumplings. It's very tough. It's really good."**

# Materials


## Main material

Material|Quantity
--|--
Rat grass|500 grams
Rice Flour|1000 grams

## accessories

Material|Quantity
--|--
Dietary alkali|Appropriate amount
Edible oil|Appropriate amount

Property|--
--|--

time consuming|Hours

# Steps

## step 1:

Fresh koji grass picked in the countryside

![step 1]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/1.jpg)

## step 2:

Wash koji grass thoroughly

![step 2]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/2.jpg)

## step 3:

Boil with appropriate amount of edible soda

![step 3]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/3.jpg)

## step 4:

Wash the boiled grass several times to remove bitterness and astringency.

![step 4]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/4.jpg)

## step 5:

Make mud with a cooking machine and drain slightly, leaving the drained water to be used.

![step 5]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/5.jpg)

## step 6:

Put crushed koji grass into glutinous rice flour, add a little edible alkali and oil

![step 6]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/6.jpg)

## step 7:

Knead into a smooth dough, a little more time can increase the toughness of the finished product.

![step 7]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/7.jpg)

## step 8:

Squeeze into 5 grams or so of small crowds, as evenly as possible.

![step 8]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/8.jpg)

## step 9:

To form a small ball

![step 9]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/9.jpg)

## step 10:

Steam in a steamer for 25 minutes

![step 10]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/10.jpg)

## step 11:

Ripe

![step 11]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/11.jpg)

## step 12:

Loading.Good dumplings can be eaten directly or cooked or fried with vegetables.

![step 12]({{site.baseurl}}/img/451183/12.jpg)
